About This App & Eye Blink Morse

This web application provides a unique way to interact with Morse code. It features real-time eye blink detection to translate your blinks into Morse code, which is then decoded into human-readable text.

All the heavy computational work (like facial landmark detection) is performed on the server, ensuring a smooth experience even on mobile devices.

Facts About Eye Blink Morse Code:
  • Historical Use: One of the most famous instances is that of **Jeremiah Denton**, a U.S. Navy pilot held as a prisoner of war in Vietnam. In 1966, during a forced televised interview, he famously blinked "T-O-R-T-U-R-E" in Morse code, confirming his status to U.S. intelligence.
  • Accessibility: Eye blink Morse code can be a vital communication method for individuals with severe paralysis or locked-in syndrome, allowing them to communicate with the outside world.
  • Simplicity: It relies on a binary input (blink/no blink, short/long blink), making it a fundamental yet powerful communication channel.
  • Challenges: Requires significant practice, consistent blinking, and a reliable detection system. Environmental factors (lighting, glasses) can affect accuracy.
Uses of Eye Blink Morse Code:
  • Assistive Technology: For individuals with limited motor control.
  • Emergency Communication: In situations where verbal communication is impossible.
  • Historical Significance: A testament to human ingenuity in extreme circumstances.
  • Educational Tool: A unique way to learn and interact with Morse code.
